1. Monitor Performance

Check the overall energy health of your LGU.

The Goal

The dashboard is your command center. Use it to get a high-level snapshot of your energy program's status and identify areas that need attention.

Step-by-Step Guide
  1. Navigate: Click Dashboard in the sidebar.
  2. Check KPIs: Review the key performance indicator (KPI) cards at the top. These show your total assets, identified savings, and project investments.
  3. Review Recent Activity: The "Recent Activity" table shows the latest consumption reports.
The Analysis: What to Look For
High consumption figures with low estimated savings might indicate a need for more RIOs. A high number of "Identified" RIOs with few "Ongoing" projects could signal a bottleneck in your project approval process.

2. Setup Your Organization

Define the LGU that owns all your assets and data.

The Goal

Create the LGU entity that will own all assets and data. The application filters everything based on the currently selected LGU.

Step-by-Step Guide
  • Navigate: Go to the LGUs page from the sidebar.
  • Add New: Click the + Add New LGU button.
  • Enter Details: Fill in your LGU's name and location information.
  • Select LGU: You must select your LGU from the dropdown menu in the top navigation bar to set your active context.
Why It's Critical
If you add assets and can't find them later, check if you have the correct LGU selected in the navbar. All data is partitioned by LGU.

3. Register Your Assets

Create a complete inventory of buildings and vehicles.

The Goal

Build a complete inventory of energy-consuming assets. You can't manage what you don't measure.

Step-by-Step Guide
  1. Navigate: Go to Buildings or Vehicles from the sidebar.
  2. Add New: Click the "Add New" button on the respective page.
  3. Fill Details: Enter the required information. Floor Area and Fuel Type are critical for metrics.
  4. Go Deeper: Use the MADE page for specific high-consumption equipment.
The Foundation of Metrics
An accurate inventory allows for meaningful comparisons (e.g., kWh per square meter) and is essential for identifying Significant Energy Users (SEUs).

4. Log Consumption Data

Record monthly electricity (kWh) and fuel (Liters) usage.

The Goal

Record monthly energy usage for each asset to build a historical trend. Consistent data entry is vital for identifying anomalies.

Step-by-Step Guide
  1. Navigate: Go to the Consumption page.
  2. Select Asset: Use the dropdown menu to select an asset.
  3. Review History: Check existing reports to avoid duplicates.
  4. Add Report: Enter Year, Month, and amount (kWh or Liters).
Powering the Insights
Gaps in data make trend analysis less reliable. This data powers the SEU analysis and Compliance Reports.

5. Propose Improvements (RIOs)

Turn your findings into actionable recommendations.

The Goal

Bridge the gap between observing an energy issue and creating a plan to fix it. This is where analysis becomes action.

Step-by-Step Guide
  1. Identify: Use the SEU page to find high-consumption assets.
  2. Investigate: Ask "Why?". Is it old equipment? Poor behavior?
  3. Propose: Go to RIOs and click "Add New".
  4. Quantify: Enter Estimated Cost and Savings to build a business case.
From Data to Persuasion
A good idea with numbers is persuasive. Quantifying impact makes it easier for decision-makers to approve your proposals.

6. Implement Projects (PPAs)

Bundle RIOs into funded projects and track their status.

The Goal

Bundle approved RIOs into funded projects and track their implementation from start to finish.

Step-by-Step Guide
  1. Navigate: Go to the Projects (PPAs) page.
  2. Bundle: Click "Add New Project" and select related RIOs.
  3. Track: Set status (Planned, Ongoing, Completed) and budget.
  4. Update: As work progresses, update the actual costs and status.
Closing the Loop
Linking RIOs to a PPA demonstrates that opportunities are being acted upon and provides the framework for tracking ROI.
